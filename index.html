First-Time Buyer Checklist
  • Get pre-approved before making any offers
  • Budget for land transfer tax, legal fees, and title insurance
  • Understand the Ontario First-Time Home Buyer Incentive programs
  • Never waive inspection in high-risk properties without expert guidance
  • Know your neighbourhood — infrastructure, school zones, transit access
  • Review status certificates for condo purchases before firming up
  • Plan for closing costs: typically 1.5%–4% of purchase price

  • Closing timeline flexibility can be a competitive advantage
  • Know your walk-away number before emotion takes over

Ontario Market

Power of Sale &
Distress Opportunities

Understanding distressed sale situations is a specialized skill. These opportunities carry potential — but also require careful due diligence and a structured approach.

What is Power of Sale in Ontario?

In Ontario, when a homeowner defaults on their mortgage, the lender has the right to sell the property without going through the court system — this is called Power of Sale. Unlike foreclosure (which is rare in Ontario), the lender simply seeks to recover the outstanding mortgage balance plus costs.

Power of Sale properties are often listed at or below market value because the lender's primary goal is debt recovery, not maximum profit. However, they are sold "as-is" — no representations, no warranties, no repairs.

Buyers of Power of Sale properties take on unknown risk: liens, outstanding property taxes, interior condition, and occupancy complications. Proper due diligence is not optional — it is essential.

Identify the Opportunity
Power of Sale listings are publicly listed but require knowing what to look for — specific listing language, lender-managed properties, and off-market situations through industry networks.
Due Diligence Framework
Title searches, outstanding tax review, utility arrears, and physical inspection (where permitted) are non-negotiable steps before an offer is considered.
Offer Structure on POS Properties
Lenders typically require Schedule A documentation and have strict acceptance timelines. Understanding their process and positioning your offer accordingly is critical to securing the deal.
Vacant Possession Confirmation
Occupied Power of Sale properties carry additional complexity. Confirming vacant possession arrangements before closing protects your timeline and investment.

Ontario Market

Market Watch &
Neighbourhood Intelligence

Understanding the Ontario real estate market requires looking beyond headlines. Inventory levels, price movement, and infrastructure development all affect buying and selling decisions.

Inventory & Supply
Active Listings vs. Buyer Demand
Ontario's market continues to be shaped by the balance between active inventory and qualified buyer demand. In supply-constrained markets like Brampton, Mississauga, and parts of the GTA, limited new listings continue to support price floors even when transaction volumes soften. Monitoring months-of-supply is a more reliable demand indicator than headline price data alone.
Data varies by neighbourhood — always verify with current MLS statistics.
Price Movement
Reading Price Trends Correctly
Average price data can be misleading when composition of sales shifts between property types. A decline in average price may reflect fewer detached home sales — not actual depreciation in a specific segment. Evaluating price-per-square-foot and median price changes by property type and neighbourhood gives a more accurate read of true market movement.
Work with a Realtor who reviews actual comparable data, not just aggregate reports.
Interest Rates & Affordability
Rate Environment & Buyer Qualification
The Bank of Canada's rate decisions directly impact buyer purchasing power and mortgage carrying costs. As the rate environment evolves, buyer qualification thresholds shift, affecting what segments of the market see the most activity. Stress test requirements and qualifying rates continue to be a significant factor for first-time buyers and investors structuring financing.
Speak with a licensed mortgage professional for current qualification modeling.
Infrastructure & Development
Development Zones & Long-Term Value
Infrastructure investment is one of the most reliable long-term value drivers in real estate. Transit expansions, GO station development, highway access improvements, and planned community growth zones in areas like Caledon, the Peel Region, and the broader GTA create medium and long-term capital appreciation opportunities for investors who position early and hold deliberately.
Infrastructure timelines are subject to government approvals and are not guarantees of value growth.
Market information above is general in nature and intended for informational purposes only. Conditions vary significantly by neighbourhood, property type, and timeframe. Always consult current MLS data and a licensed Realtor before making real estate decisions.
